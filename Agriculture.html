What is Agriculture?

Agriculture is the science, art, and practice of cultivating the soil, growing crops, and raising animals for food, fiber, fuel, and other products used to sustain and enhance human life. Agriculture is a foundational industry that has supported human civilization for thousands of years, transitioning from traditional, subsistence farming to modern, industrialized methods using advanced technologies like irrigation, fertilizers, genetics, and machinery.

  1. Crop cultivation (e.g., growing wheat, rice, vegetables)
  2. Animal husbandry (e.g., raising cattle, poultry, sheep)
  3. Horticulture (e.g., fruits, flowers, landscaping plants)
  4. Forestry and fishery in broader definitions
  5. Agro-industry (processing and distribution of agricultural products)
